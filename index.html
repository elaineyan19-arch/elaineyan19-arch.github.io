AI in My Workflow

I don't wait for the tools to mature.

My honest take — domain expertise is what makes AI actually useful

"I use AI to move fast. I use my domain expertise to get it right. The model gets me 70% of the way. The last 30% is still mine."

Work · 2025–present

AI wired into how I work — not bolted on

Built and integrated — not just used:

  • Data agent: Configured on Coze — connected to internal dashboards, pulling key metrics daily, surfacing trends, flagging anomalies. Delivers a morning brief so I'm acting on signals, not spending time in dashboards.
  • Custom news agent: Personalised agent filtering platform, commerce, and AI news down to what's relevant — delivered daily, no noise.
  • PRD templates: Fed my PRD templates for different business lines into Claude and Gemini. The model follows my structure, my language, my standards — not generic output.
  • Design direction: Use Claude to generate precise design briefs — layouts, content hierarchy, visual direction — before involving the design team. Cuts review cycles, makes proposals more concrete.
  • Growth strategy: Under company data compliance, co-pilot strategy using Gemini and DeepSeek. Assign the model a character and context from curated GitHub skills files, challenge with domain knowledge, cross-validate with a second model to surface gaps, then refine. AI speed + domain judgment together.
  • Domain onboarding: Use AI to get to the core of an unfamiliar business landscape — compress the time to asking the right questions.
Work · TikTok Shop, 2023

Used AI to enter a domain I knew nothing about — and ship a live product

Tasked with validating e-commerce insurance for TikTok Shop with zero insurance background. Used Claude and Gemini to rapidly build working knowledge — competitive landscape, regulatory structure, business model mechanics. Cross-checked everything with industry experts. What would have taken weeks took days.

Stayed in AI throughout: drafted the RFP with targeted questions, ran comparison analysis across partners, built flow mockups, brainstormed 3–5 year roadmaps — validated with domain experts at each stage. Led MVP with Cover Genius. Millions in GWP within months.

Honest note: Also my first real lesson in AI hallucination — confident, coherent, occasionally wrong in ways only an expert catches. Rule since: AI compresses the time. Experts close the last mile.

Personal · 2025–present

Claude as editorial partner, Stitch for design. Custom skills files give each tool the context, positioning, tone rules, and domain knowledge to work consistently without re-explaining each session.
